In the signin page, we are faced with a login form.
Since the creator of this website didn't leave any flaw that would expose this form to attacks, we're only left with brute force.

Brute force means trying every possible combination of credentials to eventually find one that works.
Normally, this could take forever to find something interesting.
However, we can speed up that process by targeting our victim in a smart way.

Since we're attacking a login form, we might want to try common passwords instead of random values.
List of already hacked passwords are easily findable on the internet, so that's what we will use.

We also don't really care about normal users, we want to get access to a privileged account.
Common privileged usernames are names like: admin, root, supervisor, administrator...

By using our brute-force script, we are able to find these working credentials:
    login: admin
    password: shadow
